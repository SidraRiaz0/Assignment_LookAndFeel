KEY INSIGHTS

Representation Gap

Despite women making up 46% of all gamers, they represent only 25% of the gaming industry workforce, highlighting a significant representation gap in game development and industry leadership roles.

Harassment Crisis

59% of women experience some form of toxicity while gaming, with 42% receiving verbal abuse and 30% experiencing sexual harassment. This creates a hostile environment that drives women away from gaming communities.

Leadership Underrepresentation

Women hold only 16% of executive positions in gaming companies, despite their significant presence as consumers and growing representation in development roles.

Positive Trends

There has been a gradual increase in women's representation in the gaming workforce from 22% in 2020 to 25% in 2024, showing slow but meaningful progress toward gender equality.

RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Strengthen Anti-Harassment Policies

Gaming platforms and companies should implement stronger reporting systems, faster response times, and more severe consequences for harassment to create safer gaming environments.

2. Increase Industry Representation

Gaming companies should actively recruit and promote women in leadership roles, create mentorship programs, and address workplace culture issues that may discourage women from pursuing gaming careers.

3. Community Education

Implement educational initiatives to raise awareness about the impact of harassment and promote inclusive gaming culture among all players.

4. Support Networks

Create and fund organizations that support women in gaming, provide safe spaces for networking, and offer resources for career development in the gaming industry.
