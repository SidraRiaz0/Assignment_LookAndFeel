ABOUT OUR
MISSION

Dedicated to creating an inclusive gaming ecosystem where women can thrive

The Women in Gaming community is built to highlight the incredible contributions of women in game development and gameplay. This platform serves as both a space for recognition and a source of inspiration for the next generation of girls interested in the gaming world.

FOUNDER PROFILE

Sidra

Founder & Community Leader

Sidra is a passionate developer relations and startup community manager. She has launched multiple communities and led Meta's Developer Relations program for South and Central Asia.

Sidra began her entrepreneurial journey with a VR gaming company, which later pivoted into a gamified health solution for Johns Hopkins Global. This innovation led to a $900k sponsorship from global partners.

As a vocal advocate for gender-inclusive spaces, Sidra has led "She Loves Data" in Singapore as a global lead and launched the Women in Tech USF chapter, growing it to 500+ members in just 6 months.

DEVELOPMENT DIARY

What I Learned

Building this website has been an incredible journey of discovery. I've gained hands-on experience with modern web development practices, from responsive design principles to data visualization techniques. The process of researching women in gaming opened my eyes to the significant challenges and opportunities in this space.

What Inspired Me

This project was inspired by a desire to spotlight underrepresented voices in gaming. I wanted to use design and storytelling to honor the contributions of women in the industry—whether they’re developers or players—and to encourage more girls to pursue careers in this space.

Responsive Design

Design Process

The design process began with analyzing the WeAreSocial gaming site to understand their visual language and user experience patterns. I focused on creating a cohesive brand identity that would resonate with the gaming community while maintaining professional credibility. The color palette and typography choices were carefully selected to reflect both the energy of gaming culture and the seriousness of the gender equality issues we're addressing.

Challenges & Solutions

One of the biggest challenges was balancing the need for engaging visual design with the presentation of serious statistical data about harassment and inequality. I solved this by creating distinct sections that allow the data to speak for itself while maintaining visual interest through thoughtful use of color and typography.
